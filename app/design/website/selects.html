How it works

The tedious first pass, already done.

Selects does the boring sorting, then hands you the few decisions that actually need an eye. Three taps and a shoot is curated.

1

It pre-picks the keepers. You just correct.

Selects groups each photoshoot, the same scene shot ten or twenty times, and pre‑picks the sharpest plus the one that's genuinely different so you don't lose the moment. Everything else is suggested for clearing. Tap a circle to flip between keep and clear. That's the whole job.

2

Remove, safely.

When you're happy, Selects shows exactly what you'll clear and the space you'll get back, then hands off to iPhone's own delete to confirm. Nothing is ever removed without your tap, and cleared photos stay in Recently Deleted for 30 days.

3

Your best few, and the space back.

A 40‑photo shoot becomes its best handful. Selects tallies what you kept, what you cleared, and the gigabytes you reclaimed, then lets you roll straight into the next shoot.

Another way

Or curate the whole library by hand.

Skip the grouping and browse everything, newest first. Tap the photos you don't want, and Selects queues them for the same safe removal. Two paths, one clean library.

Questions

Is it really free?

Yes. No account, no subscription, no ads, no upsell. Selects is free to use in full. There's an optional tip jar if you'd like to support it, nothing more.

Do my photos ever leave my iPhone?

Never. Selects reads your library on‑device using the Vision framework already on your iPhone, so your photos are never uploaded and never leave the device. No account, and no copy of your photos in the cloud.

Can it delete a photo I wanted to keep?

Selects only suggests. You confirm the removal, and it goes through iPhone's own delete, so cleared photos sit in Recently Deleted for 30 days and can be restored any time in that window.

What if I'd rather choose everything myself?

Tap Browse all photos to skip the grouping and go through your library by hand, newest first. Same safe removal at the end.

Which iPhones does it support?

Any iPhone running iOS 26 or later.

How is it different from Library Cleanup?

Same family, different job. Selects is the on‑the‑go photoshoot curator for iPhone. Library Cleanup is the deep‑clean tier for your whole Mac library, focused on reclaiming storage from duplicate photoshoots and videos.

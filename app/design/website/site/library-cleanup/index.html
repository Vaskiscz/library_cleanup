How it works

Four steps, and you're in control of every one.

STEP 1

Scan on your Mac

The app reads your Photos library locally and uses Apple's on-device Vision framework to spot look-alikes and clutter. No cloud, no queue.

STEP 2

Review by group

Burst clones, repeat clips, and screenshots are lined up side by side — with the sharpest, steadiest keeper already picked out for you.

STEP 3

It learns your taste

Every keep or discard you make quietly tunes the next round of suggestions toward the shots you actually care about.

STEP 4

Remove, safely

Confirmed removals go through macOS itself and land in Recently Deleted — recoverable for 30 days if you change your mind.

Privacy

Your photos never leave your Mac.

This isn't a policy footnote — it's how the app is built. There is no server to send anything to.

Processed on device

Every scan, comparison, and thumbnail is computed on your own machine using Apple's Vision framework. Your library is never copied off it.

No cloud, no account

Everything happens on your own Mac. No sign-up, no servers, no uploads, and no analytics on your images.

Open by design

Because nothing leaves your Mac, there's nothing to breach or sell. No accounts, no fine print.

A fair question

Why isn't it on the Mac App Store?

To do its job, Library Cleanup needs to read your entire Photos library and delete photos through Apple's PhotoKit. That requires Full Disk Access and delete permissions the Mac App Store sandbox simply doesn't hand out.

Rather than strip out what makes it useful to fit the sandbox, it's distributed directly.

And because it asks for that much access, the full source is public — so you (or anyone) can audit the code on GitHub and see exactly what it does before trusting it with your library.

One honest heads-up

This is a free passion project without a paid Apple Developer account, so the app is signed but not notarized. macOS will warn you the first time you open it. That's expected — the two-minute install steps right below walk you through it.

Installing

Two honest minutes to set up.

Because the app isn't notarized, macOS asks you to allow it once. Here's the whole process.

1

Open the .dmg

Drag Library Cleanup into your Applications folder.

2

Allow it once

System Settings ▸ Privacy & Security ▸ Open Anyway, then open it again.

3

Grant Full Disk Access

Privacy & Security ▸ Full Disk Access ▸ add Library Cleanup. Required to read your library.

4

Allow Photos

The first time you confirm a removal, macOS asks for Photos access. Click Allow.

FAQ

The questions worth asking.

Does it also delete from iCloud?

If you use iCloud Photos, removals sync across your devices like any deletion you make in Photos. That's exactly why everything goes to Recently Deleted first: you get 30 days to change your mind, on every device.

Is it really free? What's the catch?

Really free. No trial, no locked features, no subscription. I built it for my own library and decided to share it. If it saves you time, a tip is appreciated, but that's the entire business model.

Why does macOS warn me on first open?

The app is signed but not notarized, which requires a paid Apple Developer account this free project doesn't have. The warning is macOS being properly cautious about apps from outside the App Store. Allow it once and it won't ask again.

Will it run on an Intel Mac?

Not yet. Current builds are Apple Silicon only (M1 and newer), on macOS 13 Ventura or later.

What does it never do?

It never uploads anything, never edits or re-compresses your originals, never deletes without your confirmation, and never phones home. The source is public, so you don't have to take my word for it.
